Reime, Lara. 2025. Becoming In/Fertile:
Data Practices and Reproductive Re-con-figurations.
IT University of Copenhagen. Link to dissertation

Becoming in/fertile explores how bodies, temporalities, and orientations become figured, configured, and reconfigured within everyday practices of fertility sensemaking. I develop a qualitative analysis of fertility sensemaking that is grounded in interdisciplinary engagements with work in feminist theory, Human-computer interaction, and Science & Technology Studies, and anchors in theories on posthumanism and crip/queer temporalities.

I build on a range of empirical material, including bodily experiences around data obtained through mundane reproductive technologies, such as Menstruation and Fertility Tracking Applications (MFTAs), online forums, as well as medicalized datafication practices. Rather than only being a ‘quality of the body’, this project brings forth a conception of fertility as entangled, material, and relational practices.
